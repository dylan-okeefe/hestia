# Hestia v0.10.0 — Telegram voice hardening, Discord voice abandoned, ADR reorganization

**Released:** 2026-04-22
**Tag:** `v0.10.0` (annotated) on `develop` tip.
**Prior release:** `v0.9.1` (2026-04-20).
**Merge train:** `chore/telegram-voice-enablement-2026-04-22`.

This release hardens the Telegram voice-message pipeline, officially
abandons the Discord live-voice Phase B experiment, and reorganizes all
architecture decisions into a clean, linkable index.

Full change list lives in `CHANGELOG.md` under `[0.10.0]`.

---

## What's new

### Telegram voice hardening

- **Sub-word audio guard.** Accidental tap-and-release voice notes (<0.25 s)
  are now rejected before they reach Whisper STT, preventing nonsense
  hallucinations and wasted GPU cycles. The user gets a polite "I didn't
catch that — could you speak a little longer?" prompt.

### Discord voice abandoned

- The always-listening Discord voice channel (Phase B) is removed from dev
  (~1,100 lines deleted). The STT/TTS pipeline it spawned lives on as the
  engine for Telegram voice messages. See ADR-026 for the rationale.

### Documentation

- **ADR reorganization.** The 550-line `docs/DECISIONS.md` monolith is split
  into 26 individual ADR files (`docs/adr/ADR-001` through `ADR-026`) with
  consistent 3-digit numbering, status badges, and cross-links.
- All references updated across README, CHANGELOG, guides, releases, and
  handoffs.
- New `hestia-voice` skill documents the voice pipeline, DAVE encryption,
  and turn-detection architecture.

### Reliability

- `memory.save()` warns when called outside an identity context, preventing
  orphan unscoped rows.

---

## Upgrade notes

No database migrations required. Operators who were experimenting with
Discord voice should remove `discord_voice` from their `config.py`;
`HestiaConfig` no longer accepts that field.
